Time Line

  • January 30, 1925
    He was an American inventor of Norwegian descent. He is best known for inventing the mouse,2 and was a pioneer of human interaction with computers, including hypertext and networked computers.3 His vision assigned for Xerox PARC engineers to ultimately arrive at a better mouse design , used by the Xerox Alto, the first personal computer with a graphical interface.4
  • 1957
    He entered the Stanford Research Institute as a researcher and in 1959 rose to the position of Director of the Augmentation Research Center, which he founded.

“Society is very complex, and it is becoming more and more complicated. And the collective capacity of people to deal with this increasingly complex reality is not growing, to the point that there may come a time when it completely overwhelms us, that the institutions we have cannot face the changes with the speed and capacity necessary.” By Douglas Carl Engelbart
